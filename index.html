What Makes PFC Different from Visual Cortex? A Cell-Type View from the Kriegstein Lab's Data

Cathy Liu · cathyliu014@gmail.com · personal exploratory project, July 2026
cortical arealization single-cell transcriptomics radial glia human cortex development MAST Python

The Kriegstein lab has spent years working out how the human neocortex acquires its regional identity — why the prefrontal cortex (PFC) develops differently from the visual cortex (V1), and when those differences emerge during gestation. Their 2021 Nature cortical arealization paper (Bhaduri et al., 2021) showed that areal identity is encoded early, in progenitor cells, before the neurons even finish migrating. The lab released the full marker gene table from that paper on GitHub.

I re-analyzed the MAST differential expression results from that repo to ask: Which genes most reliably distinguish PFC from other cortical areas, and do these markers behave the same way across the three major progenitor and neuron classes?

What this is. Personal exploratory re-analysis of published data, not novel research. MAST marker table downloaded directly from github.com/kriegstein-lab/cortical-arealization (25,071 rows; Bhaduri et al. 2021). Markers are defined by significant log fold change of PFC vs. all other profiled areas (motor, parietal, somatosensory, temporal, V1), MAST test, p_adj < 0.05. Three cell types: radial glia (RG), intermediate progenitor cells (IPC), neurons.

Each cell type has a distinct set of PFC-enriched genes

HOPX — an outer radial glia marker — is enriched in PFC radial glia (logFC = 0.80, p_adj = 0.034). HOPX marks outer radial glia (oRG), a basal progenitor subtype dramatically expanded in the human cortex relative to rodents. oRG cells are thought to be particularly abundant in PFC, driving the disproportionate expansion of frontal areas during human evolution. Bhaduri et al. directly mapped this: PFC-enriched RG identity is one of the earliest molecular signatures distinguishing frontal from occipital cortex.
NEUROG1 (logFC = 1.07) is one of the strongest PFC markers specifically in early neurons. NEUROG1 is a proneural basic helix-loop-helix transcription factor that drives the transition from progenitor to neuron. Its PFC enrichment in neurons — but not in RG — suggests that frontal cortex produces early-born neurons with a distinct transcriptional identity, before the laminar migration program even completes. This is consistent with the idea that areal identity is "pre-programmed" in progenitors and transmitted to their neuronal daughters.
IPC markers show a different profile: histone variants (HIST1H4C, H2AFZ) dominate the PFC signal in IPCs. Intermediate progenitor cells bridge the RG and neuron stages. The enrichment of histone genes suggests chromatin accessibility differences in PFC-versus-other IPCs — a potential link to the 3D genome organization work the lab has been pursuing in more recent papers on cortical neurogenesis. The accessibility of different chromatin states in IPCs could determine which gene programs are opened in newly-born neurons.

PFC enrichment is established early — but genes differ in how they change over time

HOPX shows increasing PFC enrichment from early (0.80) to mid-stage (0.90) — it is not a transient early marker. This rising trajectory suggests HOPX-positive oRG cells in PFC expand or become more transcriptionally distinct mid-gestation. It is consistent with the idea that outer radial glia-driven expansion is a continuing process, not just a one-time specification event.
CLU (Clusterin) peaks early (logFC = 0.92) and falls off significantly by late stage (0.36). CLU encodes a secreted glycoprotein with roles in stress response and cell survival. Its sharp early enrichment in PFC radial glia, followed by decline, points to a transient identity signature — possibly related to PFC-specific niche signaling in the early ventricular zone that becomes less relevant as the progenitor pool depletes. The contrast between CLU and HOPX trajectories illustrates that "PFC identity" is not a single state but an evolving program.

Connection to the lab's work

  • Cortical arealization (Bhaduri et al. 2021): This re-analysis extends the paper's areal marker analysis by examining temporal dynamics and asking which markers are early-and-transient vs. maintained across stages — a distinction the paper does not highlight explicitly but that is derivable from the released MAST data.
  • 3D genome / chromatin during neurogenesis: The IPC histone variant signature suggests a chromatin accessibility angle that connects to the lab's newer work on 3D genome organization in cortical progenitors. The question of which regulatory elements are opened in PFC-vs-other IPCs seems like a natural follow-up that would require the kind of paired snRNA-seq/snATAC-seq or Hi-C profiling the lab now routinely does.
  • Spatial transcriptomics: These bulk MAST markers don't resolve spatial position within a cortical area. Mapping HOPX and NEUROG1 expression gradients across the VZ/SVZ at spatially resolved resolution — using the spatial tools the lab has been developing — would let you ask whether PFC enrichment reflects subregional differences within PFC, or a uniform PFC-wide signature.
  • On the experimental side, I have hands-on experience with mammalian cell culture, fluorescence microscopy, flow cytometry, and RNA/protein-level assays. I've also applied scRNA-seq analysis computationally. I'm comfortable contributing across both experimental and computational aspects.

Methods

Data retrieved July 2026 from the Kriegstein lab GitHub repository (cortical-arealization), file neocortex/markers/tbls_in/markers_by_celltype_indiv_all_MAST.txt (25,071 rows). Markers selected by: area = "pfc", p_val_adj < 0.05, max avg_logfc per gene per cell type per stage. Cell types: radial glia (rg), intermediate progenitor cells (ipc), neurons (neuron). Developmental stages: early, mid, late. Non-coding RNA and mitochondrial gene prefixes (MT-, AC0, MIR, LINC) excluded from Figure 1 for clarity. Figures: pure Python SVG. Code: fetch_data.py, make_figures.py. Raw tables: pfc_markers_early.tsv, stage_dynamics.tsv.
